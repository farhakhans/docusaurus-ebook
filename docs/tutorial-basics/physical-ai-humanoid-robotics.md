# Chapter 1: Introduction to Physical AI

Physical AI refers to artificial intelligence systems that interact directly with the physical world. Unlike traditional AI, which works primarily with data in digital space, Physical AI integrates **sensors, actuators, and computational intelligence** to perform real-world tasks.  

Humanoid robots are a major application of Physical AI. These systems are designed to replicate human behavior, allowing machines to navigate, perceive, and act in complex environments. Physical AI combines robotics, machine learning, and human-computer interaction to create autonomous agents capable of learning and adapting.  

Applications include robotic assistants in healthcare, delivery systems, and research platforms for AI-human interaction. The field is growing rapidly due to advances in sensors, computing, and AI algorithms, making robots smarter, safer, and more efficient in performing real-world tasks.





# Chapter 2: Sensors and Perception

Perception is crucial for humanoid robots. Sensors allow robots to gather information about their surroundings. Common sensors include **cameras, LIDAR, ultrasonic sensors, and tactile sensors**.  

Camera systems enable visual recognition, object detection, and navigation. LIDAR and ultrasonic sensors help robots measure distances, detect obstacles, and map environments. Tactile sensors simulate human touch, providing feedback for grasping objects safely.  

Data from these sensors is processed using AI algorithms to create a model of the environment. This sensory integration allows humanoid robots to interact with humans and objects more naturally, improving autonomy and decision-making.


# Chapter 3 - Actuators and Motion Control


Actuators are the muscles of robots. They convert electrical signals into physical motion. Common actuators include **motors, servos, and pneumatic systems**.  

Motion control algorithms coordinate actuators to produce smooth, human-like movements. Advanced techniques like **inverse kinematics** and **trajectory planning** allow robots to reach targets, manipulate objects, and maintain balance.  

Humanoid robots require precise control to navigate uneven terrain, perform delicate tasks, and respond to dynamic environments. Combining actuators with sensory feedback ensures that robots move safely and effectively.
